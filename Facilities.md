This page is all about EARS's facilities

# The Shack
The Shack is the EARS amateur radio lab, located at 46AB05 (on the top floor of AB, just outside the AA/AB lift door). It is accessible by all full EARS members 24/7 via RFID access, upon completion of our Shackcess health and safety protocol test.

We are currently in the process of upgrading the Shack from a mixed-use radio and electronics workshop to a dedicated RF lab.

##Electronics
For electronics users, there are two electronics benches furnished with equipment such as power supplies, multimeters, oscililloscopes, function generators, soldering irons, and even a spectrum analyser. PCs are provided for programming microcontrollers, and a range of tools for electronics construction and casework are on hand.

# The Makerspace
The Makerspace is our newest and most well-equipped facility, located at 18AB04 (the fourth floor of AB, just inside the EE undergraduate labs by the AA/AB stairwell). It is accessible by all full EARS members via swipe card access, upon completion of our Makersafe health and safety protocol test.

The Makerspace is available to EARS members as a result of a collaboration between the Electronic Engineering department and EARS. Thanks to a generous grant from the University's Annual Fund, EARS has been able to purchase modern equipment to fill the space - and is one the best-equipped electronics labs accessible to students on the campus.

## Electronics
The majority of the Makerspace is devoted to electronics use. We have a comprehensive range of modern equipment available for EARS members to use, including PCs, four-channel digital oscilloscopes, digital function generators, programmable power supplies, microcontroller programmers, and well-equipped soldering stations.

We also have Raspberry Pis and Arduinos available for integration into EARS projects, and a large supply of components available for use.

## CNC and Computer-Aided Manufacture
EARS has a 3D printer a good stock of printer filament in a range of colours The printer is a key part of many society and personal projects, and is available for members to use, once they have been trained in how to operate and maintain it correctly.

We also own our own CNC milling machine, which is due to be installed into the Makerspace in the near future and will be made accessible to our members.

EARS is also capable of providing training in Computer-Aided Design, and we hope to run a course to teach our members in the future.

## Fabrication
Part of the Makerspace is set up for fabrication, as many of our projects have a mechanical aspect to them. We have a range of hand and machine tools available to our members, including saws, drills, files and screwdrivers. We also have a good relationship with the University's mechanical workshop and can arrange for them to fabricate more complicated parts for our members.

#Want to get Involved?
To be able to make use of the Makerspace, you need to have completed our Makersafe and Shackcess course, so that we know you can use our equipment responsibly. Upon completion of the Makersafe course, you will be able to access the Makerspace using your University ID card for swipe card access.

Freshers will receive access during working hours only (9-5 weekdays) for the first semester, and then 24/7 access from then on.

In order to use the 3D printer, we ask that our members attend a training evening, because we know from experience how easy it is to break the printer - even when you know what you're doing! We will teach you how to correctly change filament reels, start and stop prints, and use the slicing program on our PCs.
